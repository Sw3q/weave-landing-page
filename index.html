The problem

01

COVID-19.

During the pandemic, we were forced into remote work. We had to adapt quickly to new tools and workflows. It was a mess.

02

Microstructures.

Microstructures are the subtle design elements shaping our interactions and are often an afterthought. Things such as the environment, participation distribution, sequence and timing, etc. With remote work, the importance of these microstructures became obvious.

03

Boring Meetings.

We've all sat through them. The ones where you leave wondering what the point was. One person talking the whole time, the everyone muted and with their cameras off. A lot of this has to do with the microstructures we use to design our interactions.

How it works

A meeting has a shape. Weave gives it one.

01

Design the meeting before it happens

You tell Weave what you're trying to achieve and who needs to be in the room. It drafts the structure: a sequence of timed phases, the prompts that frame each one, and how to configure the breakout groups.

02

The meeting runs on a script

Every phase has a duration. Weave counts down visibly. When a phase ends, participants are dispatched to their breakout groups automatically. No link-sharing. No confusion about where to go.

03

You broadcast in, from anywhere

While groups work, you can push your voice and a prompt card into every room simultaneously. They hear you. You don't have to join each room.

04

The meeting produces a document

Weave transcribes every room in real time. After the meeting, it synthesizes everything into a structured output: themes, tensions, agreements, open questions. Participants can choose to be named or anonymous.

What makes it different

Programmable breakout rooms

Groups are configured and dispatched by the system, not manually assembled during the call.

Host broadcast

Speak into every breakout room at once without joining each one individually.

Timed phase choreography

Every segment has a duration. Countdown timers are visible to all participants.

Voice modes

Read prompts live, use a generic synthesized voice, or clone your own voice for delivery.

Real-time transcription

Every room is captured, tagged by phase and group, and stored immediately.

Synthesis output

One structured document after every meeting. Themes, agreements, and open questions — ready to share.

What it's for

Built for the meetings that matter most.

Change management

When you're trying to shift how an organization thinks or works, you need everyone's voice — not just the loudest ones. Weave structures the conversation so that it surfaces what people actually believe.

Collective design

Use a Weave meeting to design a product, a process, or a strategy. The synthesis output becomes the first draft of your spec.

Organizational listening

Run a listening session that produces a real artifact. Every participant's perspective is captured, attributed the way they want, and folded into the output.
